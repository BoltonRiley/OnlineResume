International Adventures

Places I’ve been and the stories behind them

World Map

Featured Locations

France — I can speak enough French to hold a simple conversation, but I visited mostly with my family before I was old enough to fully appreciate this beautiful country. I remember the amazing food, running beside the Tour de France, and the glorious warmth of the summer sun.

China — My parents took my brother and me to see the Terracotta Warriors and the Great Wall of China, which were truly spectacular.

Fiji — As a kid I loved Fiji until we left the relaxing beaches of the resorts. Visiting smaller towns opened my eyes to the privilege I had.

Samoa — The blowholes, sliding rocks, and beaches were great fun. More importantly, the locals were incredibly happy. I had a blast losing at rugby against some of the boys we met.

New Zealand — My first solo trip. After turning 18, I took the bus to Queenstown, hiked Ben Lomond, and went bungee jumping.

Cambodia — I may be biased as this is where I met my wife and lived for six months, but Koh Rong Sanloem is heaven on earth. Skip Sihanoukville—transfer through quickly.

Russia — I eloped here. Incredible infrastructure and people. Saint Petersburg offered the best meat I’ve ever eaten, and Murmansk delivered the northern lights.

South Africa — Johannesburg was rough, but the vineyards outside Cape Town were stunning. Alto Rouge quickly became a favourite.

Indonesia (Bali) — Twist in Ubud serves some of the best food I’ve ever had. Riding dirt bikes through volcanic craters was unforgettable.

Tonga — A walking tour of a GMO company was incredibly insightful. Pangaimotu Island and Big Mama Yacht Club were highlights.

Peru — We hiked the Inca Trail to Machu Picchu during an Intrepid trip, with unforgettable stops along the way.

Ecuador (Galápagos) — A lifelong dream. Giant tortoises, blue-footed boobies, sea lions everywhere. Absolutely magical.

Turkey — Istanbul was busy, but Cappadocia at sunrise watching hot air balloons was unforgettable.

Georgia — The food here is to die for.
