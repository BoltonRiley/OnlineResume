POS Improvements

I led several POS improvements focused on reliability, efficiency, and scalability. These included implementing automated restart handling through Windows Task Manager to ensure updates and application recovery occurred correctly, and introducing consistent naming conventions across POS terminals, locations, and keyboards to improve searchability and ongoing maintenance. I also replaced paper-based communications with Microsoft Sway, giving casual staff easy access to SOPs, event details, and operational updates.

In addition, I helped roll out kiosks, self-order, self-checkout POS systems, and autonomous outlets to improve checkout speed and reduce labour requirements. A major improvement was the development of an automated POS health-check system that performed comprehensive pre-event diagnostics and delivered reports automatically. This reduced pre-event checks from up to five staff over several hours to two staff for one hour, lowering operational overheads while allowing skilled staff to focus on higher-value technical work.

Travel

I’ve been fortunate to travel widely with both family and independently, experiencing a diverse range of cultures and landscapes. Early trips to France, China (including the Great Wall and Terracotta Warriors), Fiji, and Samoa sparked an appreciation for history, food, and cultural difference, while also offering early perspective on global privilege. As I grew older, travel became more independent and adventurous, from a solo trip to Queenstown, New Zealand—hiking Ben Lomond and bungee jumping—to living in Cambodia, where Koh Rong Sanloem remains one of my favourite places in the world.

More recent travel has focused on immersive, experience-driven adventures, including hiking the Inka Trail to Machu Picchu in Peru, exploring the wildlife of the Galápagos Islands in Ecuador, and eloping in Russia, with highlights such as Saint Petersburg and seeing the northern lights in Murmansk. Other memorable experiences include vineyards near Cape Town, volcano riding in Ubud, Bali, sunrise hot air balloons in Cappadocia, Turkey, standout food in Tbilisi, Georgia, coastal exploration in Cyprus, and snowboarding in Hakuba, Japan. Across all destinations, I’ve been most drawn to meaningful local experiences, food, nature, and shared cultural moments.

Games

I’ve always been drawn to problem-solving, which naturally led to a long-standing interest in video games, board games, and interactive experiences. This interest has extended beyond playing into creating experiences for others, such as designing and hosting a multi-stage murder mystery event for friends and family. The event combined logic puzzles, physical activities, and narrative elements—including clues, red herrings, and themed challenges—and was successfully run multiple times for different groups.

This curiosity for how things work also carries into technology and game development. After selling my first business, Choo Brew Coffee, I reactivated my ABN in 2024 to start Density Games, where I began developing my own video games, including a simple RPG project. Alongside this, I enjoy both modern and classic board games such as Terraforming Mars, Heat, 500, and Settlers of Catan. I also enjoy hands-on technical projects, from experimenting with basic electronics to building and upgrading my own PC, and recently transitioning my home system to Linux.
